# BrandScript — 8 Questions

## 1. HERO — What does the customer want?

Define ONE single desire related to the brand. Not "everything" — one thing.

> Bad: "They want great service and low prices and convenience."
> Good: "A hassle-free MBA you can complete after work."

---

## 2. VILLAIN — What root cause is threatening that desire?

Must be:
- A root source (not a symptom)
- Relatable (they immediately recognize it)
- Singular (one villain only)
- Real (not manufactured fear)

> Example: Selling time-management software → villain = "time thieves" (distractions, pointless meetings)
> Example: Tesla → villain = "gas-guzzling, inferior technology"

---

## 3. PROBLEM — Three levels

- **External** (tangible barrier): What physical problem does the villain cause?
- **Internal** (emotional frustration): How does the external problem make the customer *feel*? (self-doubt, embarrassment, overwhelm, confusion)
- **Philosophical** (larger injustice): What *shouldn't* be true about this situation? (Use "ought" / "shouldn't" framing)

> Companies sell solutions to external problems. Customers buy solutions to internal problems. Always address all three.

> Example — Nespresso: External: I want better coffee at home. Internal: I want to feel sophisticated. Philosophical: I shouldn't have to be a barista.
> Example — Apple (Jobs era): External: I need a computer. Internal: I feel intimidated by technology. Philosophical: Technology should be friendly and expressive, not alienating.

---

## 4. GUIDE — How does the brand express empathy + competency?

- **Empathy statement**: Must be stated, not implied. Starts with "We understand how it feels to..." / "Nobody should have to..." / "Like you, we are frustrated by..." / "We care about your X."
- **Competency evidence** (pick any combination): testimonials, statistics ("125,000 customers trust us"), awards, press mentions, logos of known clients

> Don't put customers in the mind-reading business. If you care, say it.

---

## 5. PLAN — 3-step process or agreement list

- **Process plan** (pre- or post-purchase steps, 3–6 steps max):
  > "1. Schedule a call. 2. We build a custom plan. 3. Execute together."
- **Agreement plan** (list of promises that remove fear):
  > CarMax: no haggling, quality certification, transparent pricing, no lemon guarantee
- Give the plan a name ("The Easy Start Plan," "Our Quality Guarantee")

> The plan removes cognitive dissonance. "Let me think about it" = unresolved cognitive dissonance. Place the stones in the creek before asking for the order.

---

## 6. CALL TO ACTION — Two kinds, both required

- **Direct CTA**: Clear, economically transactional. "Buy Now," "Schedule a Call," "Get Yours Now." Never use "Learn More" or "Get Started" — too passive.
- **Transitional CTA**: Lower-commitment. Download a guide, watch a video, get a free assessment. Keeps reluctant buyers in the relationship.

> Repeat the direct CTA at top-right AND center-above-fold. Same color, same appearance every time. People scan, not read.

---

## 7. FAILURE — What does the customer lose by NOT buying?

Name the specific negative stakes. Use this four-step fear appeal:
1. They are vulnerable to a threat
2. They should act to reduce vulnerability
3. A specific action protects them
4. Challenge them to take that action

> Fear is salt — a pinch, not the whole shaker. When you're slightly uncomfortable with how negative it is, add a little more, then stop.

> Example (pest control): "Nearly 30% of all homes have evidence of termite infestation. Since termites destroy your investment, you should act now. At Acme, we offer complete home treatment — call today for 10% off."

> Loss aversion principle: People are 2–3× more motivated to avoid a loss than to achieve a gain (Kahneman, Prospect Theory).

---

## 8. SUCCESS — What does the customer's life look like after?

Fill in the Before/After grid:

| | BEFORE | AFTER |
|---|---|---|
| What do they have? | | |
| What are they feeling? | | |
| What's an average day like? | | |
| What is their status? | | |

Success should resolve all three problem levels (external + internal + philosophical) in one vision.

Three success types — pick what fits:
- **Status / power**: Access, scarcity, premium tier, identity association ("Emerald Club member")
- **Completeness**: Reduced anxiety, reduced workload, more time
- **Self-realization**: Inspiration, acceptance, transcendence

> Be specific. "A shining city on a hill" works. "A highly competitive space program" does not. Kennedy said "We're going to put a man on the moon." Vision must be visual and defined.
