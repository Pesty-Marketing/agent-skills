# Scoring Checklist — Write Mode

Use for manual spot checks or autoresearch loops. Score each output against all applicable checks. Pass = yes, Fail = no.

## Universal checks (all surfaces)

1. **Hero framing** — Does the copy keep the customer as the hero throughout? No brand-as-hero language ("We've been serving customers since..." / "Our mission is...").

2. **Internal problem named** — Does the copy explicitly name the emotional/internal problem (embarrassment, anxiety, frustration) — not just the external one?

3. **Empathy stated explicitly** — Is there a stated empathy phrase using language like "We understand...", "Nobody should have to...", or "We know how it feels to..."? Implied empathy does not count.

4. **Specific CTA** — Does every call to action use a specific verb phrase ("Get a Free Quote," "Schedule Your Inspection")? No "Learn More," "Get Started," "Explore," or "Discover."

5. **Success vision concrete** — Is there a specific, visual description of the customer's life after purchase ("Sleep through the night without hearing scratching in the walls")? Not vague outcomes ("better results," "peace of mind").

## Homepage-specific (add when scoring homepage output)

6. **Plan present** — Is there a visible 3-step process that tells the customer exactly what happens when they buy or sign up?

---

## Test Inputs

Standard inputs for running spot checks or the autoresearch loop. Use at least 2 per session.

- "Write homepage copy for a pest control company in Phoenix"
- "Write a one-liner for a residential pest control service"
- "Write a 3-email drip sequence for a home service company"

---

## Scoring notes

- 3–6 checks is the sweet spot. More than 6 and the skill starts gaming the checklist.
- All checks are yes/no only — no partial credit, no 1–10 ratings.
- A passing score is 80%+ (4/5 universal, or 5/6 with homepage check). Below that, the skill needs refinement.
- When running the autoresearch loop: change one thing in SKILL.md or a references/ file, re-run all test inputs, compare score. Keep the change if score holds or improves; revert if it drops.
