# Scoring Checklist — Write Mode

Use for manual spot checks or autoresearch loops. Score each output against all applicable checks. Pass = yes, Fail = no.

## Universal checks (all surfaces)

1. **Change in the opening** — Does the first sentence (or first paragraph at the latest) name an unexpected change, or the threat of one? No steady-state openings.

2. **Specific sacred flaw** — Is the protagonist's flawed theory of control identifiable from the copy? Is it shown through behaviour rather than announced as adjective? "He was a perfectionist" fails. "He counted the receipts three times before the customer asked for a manager" passes.

3. **Hero-maker beat present** — Is there a scene or moment showing the flaw *working* before it breaks? If the flaw never paid off, the reader has no investment in its loss.

4. **Story event strikes the specific flaw** — Does the inciting event hit the protagonist's particular theory of control, not just shock them generally? A generic crisis is not a Storr story event.

5. **Act IV present** — Is the pain of change shown, not skipped? The dark night. The moment of retreat. Without this, the transformation reads as unearned.

6. **God moment is concrete** — Does the ending deliver a specific, visual image of restored control — not a generic "they're better now" / "the company is thriving"?

7. **Brand stays the agent, not the hero** — In customer-facing or business copy: is the product/brand present as the lever the protagonist uses, not as the savior who solves it for them?

---

## Test inputs

Standard inputs for running spot checks or the autoresearch loop. Use at least 2 per session.

- "Write a founder narrative About page for a pest control company"
- "Write a case study about a homeowner who finally solved a recurring rodent problem"
- "Write a long-form opener (first 150 words) for a blog post about why most termite treatments fail"

---

## Scoring notes

- 7 checks total. Aim for 6/7 minimum. Below 5/7 means the skill or the draft needs work.
- All checks are yes/no — no partial credit.
- When the failure is "story event too generic," return to Step 7 of the worksheet. When the failure is "no Act IV," return to Step 8.
- When running the autoresearch loop: change one thing in SKILL.md or a references/ file, re-run all test inputs, compare score. Keep the change if score holds or improves; revert if it drops.

## What this skill is NOT scored against

These belong to other skills:

- **Marketing structure** (CTA placement, plan, lead generator) → the storybrand skill's audit
- **Voice and line quality** (cadence, banned phrases, surprise) → the ann-handley skill's audit
- **SEO / keyword presence** → not in scope for narrative copy

Don't try to score this skill on those criteria. Different brain mode, different tool.
