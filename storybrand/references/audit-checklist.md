# Audit Checklist

For each failure: state what is broken, quote the offending copy, suggest the exact fix.

---

## Hero check
- [ ] Is the customer positioned as the hero? Is the brand positioned as the guide?
- [ ] Is there ONE clear desire stated for the customer?
- [ ] Does any copy talk about the brand's mission, backstory, or achievements without connecting it to customer benefit? (Move to Junk Drawer)

## Villain/Problem check
- [ ] Is there a relatable villain named (root source, singular, real)?
- [ ] Are all three problem levels addressed (external, internal, philosophical)?
- [ ] Is the internal problem (emotion/frustration) front and center — not buried under the external?

## Guide check
- [ ] Does the copy express empathy *explicitly* — stated in words, not implied?
- [ ] Is there visible evidence of competency (testimonials, stats, awards, press, logos)?

## Plan check
- [ ] Is there a 3-step plan that removes cognitive dissonance?
- [ ] Does the customer know EXACTLY what happens when they buy or sign up?

## CTA check
- [ ] Is there a direct CTA (not "learn more" or "get started" or "explore")?
- [ ] Is there a transitional CTA for customers not ready to buy?
- [ ] Is the direct CTA button a distinct color, repeated consistently?

## Stakes check
- [ ] Are negative consequences of NOT buying named specifically?
- [ ] Is the fear level calibrated — present but not excessive?

## Success check
- [ ] Is there a clear, specific vision of the customer's life after purchase?
- [ ] Does it close all three story loops (external + internal + philosophical)?
- [ ] Is the vision specific and visual — or vague and abstract?

## Grunt test (5-second rule)
- [ ] Can someone who has never heard of this brand understand the offer within 5 seconds?
- [ ] Is every sentence either advancing the story or dead weight?

---

## Key Rules (Never Violate)

- **"If you confuse, you lose."** Every word earns its place or gets cut.
- **Customer = hero. Brand = guide.** The hero is always the person pulling out their wallet. Brands that position themselves as hero are destined to lose (see: Tidal).
- **Internal problems > external problems.** Companies sell solutions to external problems. Customers buy solutions to internal problems.
- **One villain only.** Multiple villains dilute focus and confuse the story.
- **Fear is salt.** Use negative stakes, but stop when you're slightly uncomfortable. Never fearmonger.
- **People don't read — they scan.** Short. Bold. Repeated CTAs.
- **Empathy must be stated, not implied.** Don't put customers in the mind-reading business.
- **A story has to go somewhere.** Always show the transformation. Never assume customers know where your brand will take them. Tell them explicitly.
- **The plan removes cognitive dissonance.** "Let me think about it" = unsolved confusion. Place the stones before asking for the order.
