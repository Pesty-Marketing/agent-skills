# Scoring Checklist — Write Mode

Use for manual spot checks or autoresearch loops. Score each output against all applicable checks. Pass = yes, Fail = no.

## Universal checks (all surfaces)

1. **Reader in first paragraph** — Does the copy open with the reader's world (their problem, situation, or desire) — not with the company's story, mission, or history?

2. **So What answered** — Is the benefit to the reader explicit and specific? Not "better results" — a named, concrete outcome.

3. **No running start** — Does the copy start at the actual point? No "In today's world…" / "It's no secret that…" / "As we all know…" openers?

4. **No banned words** — Zero instances of: synergy, cutting-edge, revolutionary, world-class, leverage, impactful, seamless, game-changing, bandwidth, learnings, or Frankenwords (-ization, -ification where simpler form exists)?

5. **Show don't tell** — Every adjective claim is supported by a concrete example, number, or sensory detail?

## Structural checks (blog posts, emails, longer copy)

6. **Lead hooks** — Does the opening give a reason to keep reading within the first 2 sentences?

7. **Kicker lands** — Does the ending close the loop with a clear next step or resonant final line (not just trailing off)?

---

## Test Inputs

Standard inputs for running spot checks or the autoresearch loop. Use at least 2 per session.

- "Write the hero section of a homepage for a pest control company"
- "Write an About Us page for a small home services company"
- "Write a welcome email for a new customer of a lawn care company"
- "Write a blog post intro about why DIY pest control fails"

---

## Scoring Notes

- 5 universal checks = baseline. 80%+ (4/5) is passing.
- Add structural checks when scoring longer-form content. 6/7 = passing.
- Banned-words check is a hard fail — any single banned word fails the check.
- When running the autoresearch loop: change one thing in SKILL.md or a references/ file, re-run all test inputs, compare score. Keep if score holds or improves; revert if it drops.
