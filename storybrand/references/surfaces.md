# Output Surfaces

## Homepage / Landing Page (8-section wireframe)

Apply the BrandScript in this section order:

1. **HEADER (above fold)**: Large-text offer + direct CTA (top-right) + direct CTA (center) + optional transitional CTA beside it
   - Must do ONE of: promise aspirational identity, promise to solve a problem, or state exactly what you do
   - Bad: "We've been in business since 1979, committed to excellence..." Good: "We Will Make You a Pro in the Kitchen"
   - Text bold and short. Images = happy people who have solved their problem. NOT buildings, staff, or office interiors.

2. **STAKES**: Negative consequences of NOT doing business with you. Near the top — problem is the hook. Paragraph, bullets, or video.

3. **VALUE PROPOSITION**: Swing back positive. Slightly expanded version of the header offer. Paragraph, bullets, or testimonial.

4. **GUIDE**: Introduce the brand as guide. Express empathy + show competency (testimonials, stats, awards, logos). Do NOT talk about the brand as hero here.

5. **PLAN**: 3-step process plan. Removes cognitive dissonance — customer needs to see the steps before clicking Buy.

6. **EXPLANATORY PARAGRAPH**: 2–4 paragraphs for customers doing due diligence. Goes deeper on the offer.

7. **LEAD GENERATOR**: Repeat the transitional CTA in the body (not just a pop-up). Offer something specific — not "subscribe to our newsletter."

8. **JUNK DRAWER** (footer): Everything that previously cluttered the page — backstory, awards, links, legal.

**Word count rule**: People scan, not read. Ten sentences or fewer on the whole page if possible. Use a "read more" expander for longer sections. Default exercise: cut half the words from any existing page.

**CTA placement rule**: Direct CTA appears at top-right AND center-above-fold. Same button color (brighter than any other button on the page), same label, repeated every major section.

## One-Liner (elevator pitches, hero sections, taglines)

Three parts:
1. **Problem** (the hook — opens the story): "Many people struggle with X..."
2. **Product as solution** (short — stop talking): "That's why we created [Name]."
3. **Result** (vision of success): "So you can [specific outcome]."

> Bad: "Our new toothpaste is gentle on teeth and gums."
> Good: "Many people have sensitive teeth and experience pain when brushing. Our toothpaste is gentle on teeth and gums, so you can brush without pain."

> Keep editing until strangers ask for your business card. First draft is always rough.

## Email / Drip Sequences

Three stages of the customer relationship — content must match the stage:

- **Curiosity collateral** (landing pages, ads, webinars): piques interest, opens the story loop
- **Enlightenment collateral** (PDFs, email sequences, videos): builds trust, closes cognitive dissonance
- **Commitment collateral** (sales emails, countdown timers, special offers): asks for purchase

Nurture emails → provide genuine value, tell customer transformation stories.
Sales emails → direct CTA, clear stakes, short.
